## Overview

The **WHOLE NUMBER Strategy** is a price action trading methodology for Bitcoin (BTC) that focuses on psychological price levels at thousand-dollar increments (whole numbers) and specific coordinate points within each thousand-dollar range.

**Key Philosophy:**
- "Reading humanity" - understanding psychological behavior at key price levels
- Price action over technical indicators
- Time cycles and market rhythm
- Walk-away method to avoid time paralysis

## Core Concept

### What are Whole Numbers?

Whole numbers are major psychological price levels at thousand-dollar increments:
- Examples: $95,000, $96,000, $97,000, $118,000, $119,000
- These act as strong support/resistance zones
- Market participants tend to react predictably near these levels

### The Strategy's Foundation

1. **Direction determines position**
   - Long (buy) BELOW whole numbers when direction is bullish
   - Short (sell) ABOVE whole numbers when direction is bearish

2. **Never chase**
   - Don't long at fresh whole numbers (e.g., 119,100)
   - Don't short immediately when whole numbers break
   - Wait for dips and pumps

3. **Velocity matters**
   - Strong velocity = price shoots through levels quickly
   - Weak velocity = price barely crosses and falls back

## The Number System

### Middle Numbers (Neutral Zone)
**The 500s, 400s, and 600s**
- Examples: $117,500 / $117,400 / $117,600
- Indicates indecision
- Direction not yet determined
- Wait for confirmation before entering

### Acceleration Numbers (Bullish)
**The 900s**
- Examples: $117,900, $118,900
- Signals acceleration toward next whole number
- Needs to HOLD this level
- If it holds, likely to break to next thousand

### Breaking Down Numbers (Bearish)
**The 300s and below**
- Examples: $117,300, $117,200
- Indicates weakness
- Potential breakdown to lower whole number

### The BEAMS (Critical Breakdown Points)
These are the specific coordinates that signal a whole number WILL break:

1. **226** - First warning
2. **113** - Second warning  
3. **086** - The sledgehammer (definitive break signal)

**Example:** If at $117,000 level:
- $117,226 breaks - first beam
- $117,113 breaks - second beam
- $117,086 breaks - like a "sledgehammer on ice"
- After 086 breaks, the whole number WILL break

**Important:** Don't short immediately when 086 breaks - use the "Dwarf Tossing" technique (see Advanced Techniques)

## Entry & Exit Strategies

### 1. The Box Trade

Create a "box" around the whole number:

**Setup:**
- **Bottom of box:** Small position ($500) below whole number as insurance
- **Top of box:** Main position ($1,300+) above whole number for real trade

**Example at $95,000:**
- Bottom: $94,800 - $500 long position (won't miss the move)
- Top: $95,123 - $1,300 short position (main trade)

**Benefits:**
- Covered both directions
- Small loss on insurance position
- Catch the real move with main position

### 2. Shorting Over Whole Numbers (Bearish)

**When to short:**
- Direction is bearish (red candles on 1H and 6H)
- Price pumps over whole number with weak velocity
- Enter above whole number: Examples: $118,329, $119,200

**Don't short when:**
- Strong green candles on 6-hour timeframe
- High velocity breakthrough
- At the fresh whole number (wait for pump)

### 3. Longing Under Whole Numbers (Bullish)

**When to long:**
- Direction is bullish (green candles on 1H and 6H)
- Price dips below whole number
- Enter in the 700-888 range below whole number

**Example:** If whole number is $119,000:
- Long at: $118,700 - $118,888
- Don't long at: $119,100 (too late, will slip down)

### 4. The 6:30 AM Low Strategy

**Pattern:**
- Around 6:30 AM (New York time), there's often a low point
- Overnight dumps typically end here
- Quick scalp opportunities
- Can produce 29%+ gains in 3 minutes

**Example from transcript:**
- Entry: $118,329 (over whole number)
- Duration: 3 minutes
- Result: 29% profit

## Advanced Techniques

### 1. Dwarf Tossing (Breaking Whole Numbers)

**When 086 breaks and whole number is about to break:**

**Steps:**
1. **Let it break first** - don't be the first to short
2. **Take screenshot** - capture how deep it goes (e.g., to 888 or 700)
3. **Wait for buy-back** - they will buy the dip (especially at 888-700 range)
4. **Short the pump** - when it pumps back up with low velocity
5. **Use your map** - you know how deep it can go from screenshot

**Why it works:**
- Always breaks again after first break
- You have a "map" of the depth
- Low velocity pump = easy short
- Can hold longer knowing the depth

**Example:**
- $117,000 breaks, goes to $116,888
- Take screenshot showing $116,888 low
- Wait for buy-back to $117,200
- Short at $117,200 (weak pump)
- Hold to $116,700-$116,888 (your map)
- Result: 30-40% at 86X leverage

### 2. The Walk-Away Method

**Purpose:** End screen slavery and time paralysis

**How it works:**
1. Set up trade with proper $3,000 gap
2. Set limit orders if desired
3. **Walk away** - don't stare at every tick
4. Use Coinbase 1-hour chart in hand (mobile)
5. Exchange makes audible "chime" when order fills
6. Glance at chart periodically
7. Return when action is needed

**Benefits:**
- Avoid emotional decisions
- No time paralysis
- Clear mind for assessment
- Better trade execution
- Less stress

**Rule:** Once you have the gap, you can walk away confidently

### 3. Velocity Reading

**High Velocity:**
- Price shoots through whole number
- Goes 300-500+ points beyond
- Strong momentum
- Likely to continue

**Low Velocity:**
- Barely crosses whole number
- Goes only 50-200 points beyond
- Falls back quickly
- Weak momentum
- Good for reversal trades

**How to use:**
- Screenshot first break
- Measure how far it went
- Low velocity = wait for pump to short (or dip to long)
- High velocity = don't fight the trend

### 4. Scaling Into Trades

**Never go all-in at once**

**Proper scaling:**
1. First position: 20-30% of intended trade size
2. Check gap: Still $3,000 away from liquidation?
3. Add more: Another 20-30%
4. Reassess gap again
5. Continue until full position but ALWAYS maintain $3,000 gap

**Key:** The gap percentage varies with your wallet size and position size

## Glossary
